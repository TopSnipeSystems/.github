# Security Policy
TopSnipe Systems is committed to protecting the security, privacy, and integrity of the organizations and communities we serve.
Our platform supports mission‑critical operations across government, nonprofit, public health, environmental, and research sectors.
For this reason, we follow strict security and responsible disclosure practices.

## Reporting a Vulnerability
If you believe you have discovered a security vulnerability in any TopSnipe Systems repository, service, or component:

Do not:
- Open a public GitHub issue
- Discuss the vulnerability publicly
- Share details with anyone outside a responsible disclosure process

Instead, contact our security team directly:
security@topsnipesystems.com

Please include:
- A clear description of the issue
- Steps to reproduce (if applicable)
- Any relevant logs, screenshots, or proof‑of‑concept details
- Your contact information for follow‑up
- We will acknowledge receipt and begin investigation promptly.

## Responsible Disclosure
TopSnipe Systems follows industry‑standard responsible disclosure practices:
- We investigate all reports thoroughly
- We work with reporters to validate findings
- We remediate confirmed issues as quickly as possible
- We provide updates throughout the process
- We credit researchers when appropriate and permitted
- We do not pursue legal action against good‑faith security researchers who follow this policy.

## Scope
This policy applies to:
- All repositories under the TopSnipeSystems GitHub organization
- All platform components, kernels, shared libraries, and systems
- Any public‑facing services or endpoints operated by TopSnipe Systems
- Internal systems not exposed publicly are out of scope unless explicitly authorized.

## Out of Scope
The following are not considered vulnerabilities:
- Social engineering attempts
- Physical security issues
- Denial‑of‑service attacks
- Automated scanning without context
- Issues requiring privileged access to exploit
